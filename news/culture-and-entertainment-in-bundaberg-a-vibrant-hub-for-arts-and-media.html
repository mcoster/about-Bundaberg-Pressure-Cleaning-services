Culture and Entertainment in Bundaberg: A Vibrant Hub for Arts and Media

Posted by on

 

Bundaberg offers a vibrant arts and entertainment scene, making it a cultural hub in Queensland. The city features two cinemas for film enthusiasts: Reading Cinemas, located on Johanna Boulevarde in West Bundaberg, and the Moncrieff Entertainment Centre (formerly known as the Moncrieff Theatre), situated on Bourbong Street in the city center. The Moncrieff Entertainment Centre hosts live musical and theatrical performances throughout the year, making it a popular venue for both local and touring artists.

The Bundaberg Regional Art Gallery (BRAG) is a significant cultural facility that showcases a diverse range of visual arts, promoting local artists and providing a platform for exhibitions and community events. Additionally, the Bundaberg Regional Council operates a public library at 49 Woondooma Street, serving as a resource for literature and community engagement.

In terms of media, Bundaberg is served by the NewsMail, a local newspaper published from Monday to Saturday, which is available in both print and online formats. The city also has several community newspapers, including the Guardian, The Bugle, and the Bundaberg Coastline, offering additional perspectives on local news and events.

Bundaberg boasts a diverse radio landscape, featuring a mix of commercial and community stations. Notable stations include ABC Local Radio (Wide Bay 855 AM/100.1 FM), 4BU 1332 AM, Triple M 93.1, Hitz FM 93.9, and several others that provide news, music, and entertainment to residents. Commercial television services are provided by three main stations: Seven Queensland, WIN Television, and Channel 10, along with publicly owned services such as ABC TV and SBS. Local news coverage is available from all three commercial networks, with Seven News and WIN Queensland’s WIN News airing half-hour bulletins at 5:30 PM each weeknight.

Bundaberg has made its mark in popular culture, having featured in several films. Notable titles include The Delinquents (1989), starring Kylie Minogue, and The Mango Tree (1977). More recently, Talking Back at Thunder (2014), starring Steven Tandy, and the upcoming Flathead (2024), directed by Jaydon Martin, highlight the city's cinematic presence.

Overall, Bundaberg's rich cultural offerings in arts, media, and entertainment contribute to a dynamic community, providing residents and visitors with diverse opportunities for engagement and enjoyment.
